Color & Typography Decisions

April 11, 2025 Rogers Sampaio

The visual identity of a portfolio website is largely defined by its color palette and typography choices. In this article, I'll explain why I chose the Mocha Mousse color scheme and DM Sans with Playfair Display for typography, and how these decisions contribute to the overall user experience of my portfolio.

Embracing Mocha Mousse: Pantone Color of the Year 2025

When selecting a color palette for my portfolio, I wanted something contemporary yet timeless, sophisticated yet warm. The announcement of Pantone's Color of the Year 2025, Mocha Mousse (#a47864), presented the perfect opportunity to incorporate a fresh, trend-forward hue that also aligned perfectly with my design goals.

Why Mocha Mousse Works

Mocha Mousse is a warm, earthy brown with subtle reddish undertones that creates an inviting, grounded feeling. This color works exceptionally well for a portfolio for several reasons:

  • Warmth and Approachability - The warm tone feels inviting and approachable, creating an immediate positive emotional connection
  • Sophistication - Unlike brighter colors, this subdued hue communicates professionalism and sophistication
  • Versatility - It pairs beautifully with a wide range of complementary colors, allowing for a balanced palette
  • Contemporary Relevance - As the Pantone Color of the Year 2025, it positions my portfolio as current and trend-aware
  • Differentiation - It stands apart from the blues and greens commonly used in tech portfolios, helping my site be memorable

Building the Color Palette

While Mocha Mousse serves as the primary brand color, a single color isn't enough to create a complete design system. I developed a full palette around it:

  • Primary: Mocha Mousse (#a47864) - Used for primary buttons, accents, and brand elements
  • Secondary tints (#b89084, #caa89e) - Lighter variations for hover states and secondary elements
  • Complementary neutrals (#9e9286, #d4c0b9) - For backgrounds and subtle accents
  • Dark (#333333) - For text and high-contrast elements
  • Light (#f8f5f3) - For backgrounds and text on dark elements

This palette maintains cohesion while providing enough variation for different UI needs. I implemented CSS variables to manage these colors consistently throughout the site:

:root {
    /* Colors */
    --primary-color: #a47864;
    --primary-light: #b89084;
    --primary-lighter: #caa89e;
    --secondary-color: #9e9286;
    --secondary-light: #d4c0b9;
    --dark-color: #333333;
    --gray-color: #777777;
    --light-color: #f8f5f3;
    --bg-color: #ffffff;
    --light-bg: #f9f5f3;
}

Color Accessibility Considerations

While aesthetics were important, I also prioritized accessibility in my color choices. I ensured that:

  • Text colors have sufficient contrast against their backgrounds (meeting WCAG AA standards)
  • Interactive elements are clearly distinguishable through color and other visual cues
  • Color is never the sole indicator of meaning or functionality

Typography: DM Sans & Playfair Display

Typography is arguably even more important than color in establishing a website's visual identity. I chose a combination of DM Sans and Playfair Display to create a modern-meets-classical aesthetic that aligns with my overall design philosophy.

DM Sans for Readability and Modern Feel

DM Sans is a geometric sans-serif typeface with a modern, clean appearance. I selected it as my primary font for body text and UI elements for several reasons:

  • Exceptional readability - Its clean forms and proper spacing make it highly legible even at smaller sizes
  • Contemporary aesthetic - The geometric structure feels current and tech-forward
  • Character - Despite being a sans-serif, it has subtle unique characteristics that give it personality
  • Versatility - It works well across different weights, making it suitable for various UI elements

I implemented DM Sans with the following adjustments to optimize its appearance:

  • Slightly tighter letter-spacing (-0.01em) to create a more cohesive text block
  • Increased line height (1.7) for improved readability, especially in longer paragraphs
  • Strategic use of weights (400 for body text, 500 for UI elements, 600 for emphasis)

Playfair Display for Classical Elegance

For headings and statement text, I chose Playfair Display, a serif typeface with classical influences. This selection:

  • Creates visual hierarchy - The contrast between serif headings and sans-serif body text clearly defines content structure
  • Adds sophistication - The refined serifs bring a touch of elegance that elevates the overall design
  • Embodies the classical element - It represents the "classical" aspect of my modern-classical design fusion
  • Offers distinctive character - Its high contrast and unique details make headings memorable

I particularly employed Playfair Display in the hero section and major section headings to create impactful moments throughout the user journey.

Typographic Hierarchy

Beyond font selection, I established a clear typographic hierarchy to guide users through content:

  1. Primary headings - Playfair Display, 48-50px, used for the main hero text and section titles
  2. Secondary headings - Playfair Display, 30-36px, for subsection headings
  3. Tertiary headings - DM Sans, 22-24px, semi-bold, for component headings
  4. Body text - DM Sans, 16-18px, for main content
  5. Supporting text - DM Sans, 14-15px, for captions and auxiliary information

This hierarchy creates a natural flow through the content, helping users scan and digest information effectively.

Typography Implementation

The fonts are implemented through Google Fonts with appropriate weights preloaded:

<link href="https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=DM+Sans:opsz,wght@9..40,300;9..40,400;9..40,500;9..40,600;9..40,700&family=Playfair+Display:wght@400;500;600;700&display=swap" rel="stylesheet">

And managed through CSS variables for consistency:

:root {
    /* Typography */
    --classical-font: 'Playfair Display', serif;
    --modern-font: 'DM Sans', sans-serif;
}

Color and Typography in Action

The true test of color and typography choices is how they work together in the actual interface. Throughout the site, I employed these elements to:

  • Create focal points - The Mocha Mousse color draws attention to important actions and information
  • Establish rhythm - Consistent typographic spacing creates a sense of order and harmony
  • Guide user flow - Strategic color and type changes help direct the user's journey through the content
  • Maintain cohesion - The consistent palette and type system tie different sections together into a unified experience

Micro-Interactions and Hover States

I paid particular attention to how color and typography behave during interactions. For example:

  • Buttons shift to a slightly lighter shade of Mocha Mousse on hover, providing clear feedback
  • Navigation links feature subtle transitions that guide users to their current location
  • Interactive elements grow slightly and change color to indicate their actionable nature

Conclusion

The combination of Mocha Mousse as the primary color and the DM Sans/Playfair Display typographic pairing creates a visual language that is simultaneously modern, sophisticated, and timeless. These choices weren't merely aesthetic—they were strategic decisions to create a specific impression and user experience.
